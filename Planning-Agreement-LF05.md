# Learning Field 5: Database Adaptation and Deployment

**Project:** Kraut & Rüben (IT-Solutions GmbH)

## Overview of Assessment Components

The group size for this learning field is 2–3 students. Your performance and progress will be evaluated through the following milestones and assessments:

**Important:** All build and software scripts must be stored in a repository of your choice and kept up to date.

## 1. Mid-Project Checkpoint Meeting

* **Duration:** Max. 15 minutes per team
* **Grading:** Ungraded, but a mandatory milestone
* **Language:** English
* **Purpose:** To ensure your database model is on the right track and to catch potential structural errors early before you start writing complex queries.

### What you need to present:

* **Working DB Include:** You must demonstrate, that the provided database-script can be imported without syntax errors and that the tables are linked correctly (foreign key constraints are working).
* **Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD):** A clear, updated visualization of your future database structure, including the new entities/tables planned for the customer.

**Note:** Actual SQL implementation of this expanded ERD is not yet required at this stage!

## 2. Customer Meeting & Expert-Talk – 100% of Overall Grade

* **Duration:** Max. 30 minutes per team

  * **15 minutes:** Customer presentation of the results / current status – 50% of Overall Grade
  * **15 minutes:** Expert-Talk / Q&A – 50% of Overall Grade
* **Language:** English
* **Target Audience - Customer Presentation:** Keep in mind that the customer has little to no prior technical knowledge. You must translate your technical solutions into business value.
* **To be shown:** Fully implemented database solution including deployment structure (dump), SQL queries, and functional data deletion according to GDPR regulations.
* **Expert-Talk:** This part is intended to be deeply technical. Show what you have learned and prove your skills (make sure to bring all your technical artifacts/scripts with you).

## 3. SuK Team Reflection according to Belbin

* **Format:** 15-minute professional/technical discussion with the teacher about your teamwork.
* **Content:** You will talk about your estimated team roles and how your team role actually manifested during this project, backed up by specific examples. You must also describe and analyze the team roles of your partners.
* **Theoretical Framework:** The Belbin Team Role Model must be used!
* **Core question to answer:** Are you a well-functioning team?
* **Action Item:** Dates and time slots will be agreed upon directly with the teacher.

## Important Notes & Scope Clarifications

* **Source of Truth:** These requirements as described above are the ones to be fulfilled by you. The requirements described in the general "Lernfeld-Kurs" text may differ (e.g., regarding the necessity of a GUI) and are not binding for your grading. In case of doubt, ask a teacher!
* **GDPR Scope:** The topic of GDPR is not covered in depth in this learning field, as it is dealt with in detail elsewhere in your curriculum. Therefore, you do not need to write a legal paper. However, the disclosure of data and its deletion must be technically implemented in the form of an SQL script. The goal here is that the Die Erbsenzähler employees can query and delete customer data upon request, not the end-customers themselves via a self-service portal.
